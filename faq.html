Frequently Asked Questions on Golden Ratio

FAQ is a list of frequently asked questions and answers on a particular topic. Here, we have provided some of the common questions tend to occur or queries by new users related to common knowledge gaps.

Which famous quadratic equation can be used to derive the golden ratio directly?

x^2-x+1=0 
The 2 solutions to the quadratic equation x^2-x-1=0 are (1+5^0.5)/2 and (1-5^0.5)/2 respectively. The former is the golden ratio itself, namely 1.61803 while the latter gives a value of -0.61803...

The golden ratio is symbolized by which Greek letter?

The golden ratio is symbolised by phi which is a greek letter.

In what way is the golden ratio, phi, related to the Fibonacci sequence?

The ratio of each successive pair of numbers in the Fibonacci Sequence converge on the golden ratio as you go higher in the sequence.

The value of phi, namely the golden number is approximately 1.6180339887... What type of number is it?

The correct answer is An irrational number

The American mathematician, Mark Barr, has chosen Phi as the symbol for the golden ratio. Phi is named after which of the following Greek sculptors?

It has it's own story. Phidias was the sculpture after which phi was named. Phidias was a famous Greek sculptor who designed the Parthenon. It is believed that he applied the golden ratio in the building's architecture.

Nowadays, the golden ratio is symbolized by the Greek letter phi. Sometimes, it is also represented by another Greek letter. Which letter is it?

The correct answer is Tau The delta symbol is used to represent a small change of a quantity. Meanwhile, the sigma notation represents summation of a set of numbers. On the other hand, the letter lambda represents wavelength.

The golden ratio is also known by many other names. Which of the following names is NOT one of those?

Your Answer: The golden number 
The correct answer is The golden integer
Besides those names, it is also called the golden cut, the golden section and the divine proportion.

The number pi (3.142...) is celebrated on 14 March. On which day do we celebrate phi day?

Phi day is celebrated on 18 June over the whole world

The ancient Egyptians used the golden ratio in their pyramids. At that time, the golden ratio was known to them by another name, which is?

The sacred ratio 
Besides pyramids, the Egyptians also used the golden ratio in many of their other architectures (temples) and arts.
